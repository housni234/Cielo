According to the Ley De Emrendedores (2013), you can apply for the Spanish entrepreneurship visa with a business plan of a venture that intends to help the Spanish economy directly or indirectly. The original visa is one-year, but it can be extended for another two years with the development of the business. Although the process is complicated, we have witnessed a few successful cases. Although Startup Cielo doesn’t assist with the above-mentioned visas directly, we collaborate with selected few lawyers who can help with the process.

Our academic research suggests that the startup develops similar to a child, thus it needs an appropriate period to grow properly under supervision. A newborn cannot be fully autonomous and functional within 6 weeks to 5 months, neither can the first-time parents become pros during such a short period. A one-year program allows you and your startup, like a parent-child duo, to learn how to breathe, walk, nourish, and communicate in a healthy way.
